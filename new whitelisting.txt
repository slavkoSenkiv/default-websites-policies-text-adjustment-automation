<h3 id="gmail"><strong>GMAIL</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>There are three ways to whitelist Gmail:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p><strong>REMOVE AN EMAIL FROM SPAM FOREVER</strong></p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If an email is already in your primary folder but&nbsp;<strong>you want to make sure all emails from this sender always end up there, you can create a filter.&nbsp;</strong>To do this, follow these steps:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol><li>Click the&nbsp;<strong>cog icon</strong>&nbsp;in the top-right corner, and then&nbsp;<strong>Settings</strong>&nbsp;from the drop-down menu.</li><li>Click the&nbsp;<strong>Filters tab</strong>&nbsp;then&nbsp;<strong>Create a new filter</strong>.</li><li>Enter the domain of the email you want to whitelist in the&nbsp;<strong>From</strong>&nbsp;field.</li><li>Click&nbsp;<strong>Create filter with this search</strong>.</li><li>In the box titled&nbsp;<strong>When a message arrives that matches this search</strong>&nbsp;select&nbsp;<strong>Never send it to spam</strong>.</li><li>Click the&nbsp;<strong>Create filter</strong>&nbsp;button.</li></ol>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":123} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="https://wizzardofwellness.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/gmail1-1024x597.gif" alt="" class="wp-image-123"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If the email is already in your spam folder, mark it as “not spam”. Open the email in your spam folder that you want to whitelist, then click&nbsp;<strong>Report not spam</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":124} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="https://wizzardofwellness.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/gmail.png" alt="" class="wp-image-124"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>If you’re using Gmail tabs (Promotional and Social), click and drag the email you want to whitelist from the tab it’s in to the Primary tab, like so:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol><li>Gmail tabs</li><li>Marking an email as “not spam”</li><li>Creating a filter</li></ol>
<!-- /wp:list -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":125} -->
<figure class="wp-block-image"><img src="https://wizzardofwellness.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/01/gmail2.gif" alt="" class="wp-image-125"/></figure>
<!-- /wp:image -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>Once done, you’ll get an alert saying “This conversation has been moved to Primary. Do this for all future messages from sender@theirdomain.com?” Click&nbsp;<strong>Yes</strong>.</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:image {"id":126} -->

<h3 id="mac"><strong>MAC OS X MAIL APP</strong></h3>
<!-- /wp:heading -->

<!-- wp:paragraph -->
<p>To whitelist all emails from a domain in the Mail app for Mac OS X or macOS:</p>
<!-- /wp:paragraph -->

<!-- wp:list {"ordered":true} -->
<ol><li>In the top menu, click&nbsp;<strong>Mail &gt; Preferences</strong>.</li><li>Click the&nbsp;<strong>Rules&nbsp;</strong>tab.</li><li>Click&nbsp;<strong>Add Rule</strong>.</li><li>Enter a whitelist name in the&nbsp;<strong>Description</strong>&nbsp;field, such as “Whitelist: sumo.com” to create the new rule.</li><li>For conditions, set the first dropdown menu item to&nbsp;<strong>any</strong>. It should say:&nbsp;<strong>If any of the following conditions are met</strong>.</li><li>In the following dropdown menus, select&nbsp;<strong>From</strong>&nbsp;in the first field and&nbsp;<strong>Ends with</strong>&nbsp;in the second field.</li><li>In the text field following&nbsp;<strong>Ends with</strong>, enter the domain name that you want to whitelist. Include “@” before the domain name to make the filter specific—for example, to whitelist all mail from the sumo.com domain, but not mail that might come from one of its subdomains (such as @subdomain.sumo.com), type “@sumo.com” into the field.</li><li>Click the&nbsp;<strong>plus sign</strong>&nbsp;next to the last condition to add another domain with the same criteria if you want to whitelist more domains.</li><li>In the&nbsp;<strong>Perform the following actions</strong>&nbsp;section, set the three dropdown items to:&nbsp;<strong>Move Message</strong>, to the mailbox:&nbsp;<strong>Inbox</strong>&nbsp;(or any target folder you want).</li><li>Click&nbsp;<strong>OK</strong>&nbsp;to save the rule.</li></ol>
